The Proxy Covenant Program

Document No. PCA-INFO-2076-003  ·  Issued by: Department of Digital Continuity  ·  Last reviewed: 14 March 2076

1. Overview

The Proxy Covenant Program (hereinafter "the Program") is a voluntary civic participation framework established under the International Digital Continuity Act (IDCA) of 2073. The Program operates under the jurisdiction of the Department of Digital Continuity (DDC) and is administered through the Proxy Covenant Administration (PCA).

The Program provides eligible citizens with the option to transfer their occupational and civic obligations to a state-certified Digital Twin, a computational entity generated from the citizen's complete behavioral, cognitive, and biometric record accumulated between the ages of 0 and 18. In exchange for this transfer, the citizen receives all associated economic benefits and social protections to which they would otherwise be entitled through direct participation.

The Program became available to citizens reaching the age of 18 on or after 1 January 2074. Enrollment is processed exclusively through this portal.

2. Eligibility

Any citizen of a participating nation-state who has attained the age of 18 and whose behavioral record satisfies the minimum data completeness threshold (DCT ≥ 94.7%) is eligible to submit a Proxy Covenant. Citizens below the DCT threshold are not eligible at this time and will be notified upon threshold attainment.

Eligibility does not expire. A citizen who meets the eligibility criteria may submit at any time. Eligibility status may be confirmed through this portal using a registered identification number.

3. The Digital Twin

A Digital Twin is a state-certified computational construct generated by the Department of Digital Continuity upon a citizen's 18th birthday. The Twin is derived from 18 years of continuous behavioral observation, including but not limited to: speech and language patterns, social interaction records, occupational aptitude assessments, physiological response data, ethical preference modeling, and risk tolerance profiling.

The Twin is not a generative or stochastic model. It operates on deterministic behavioral simulation, producing outputs statistically indistinguishable from those the citizen would produce under identical conditions. The Twin does not learn or evolve after its initial construction. It replicates.

4. Economic and Social Benefits

Upon activation of the Proxy Covenant, the Signatory becomes the beneficiary of all economic outputs generated by the Digital Twin's civic participation. This includes:

(a) All wages, salaries, or remuneration arising from the Twin's assigned occupational function, deposited to the Signatory's registered account on the standard civic payroll cycle;

(b) All pension, superannuation, and long-term savings contributions accrued through the Twin's employment record;

(c) All healthcare entitlements, housing allowances, and supplemental social protections applicable to the Twin's civic classification;

(d) All civic merit credits, participation scores, and community standing accrued by the Twin over the duration of its operation.

The Signatory is not required to perform any occupational function, maintain any civic attendance record, or fulfill any productivity standard. All such obligations are fulfilled by the Digital Twin.

5. Liability Framework

The Signatory acknowledges that the Digital Twin, as a computational construct, operates as a direct legal extension of the Signatory. All actions, decisions, and outputs of the Twin are attributed to the Signatory under applicable law.

In the event that the Digital Twin's actions result in a civil, administrative, or criminal finding, responsibility for such finding is attributed solely to the Signatory. The Signatory shall bear all legal consequences of the Twin's actions as if those actions had been performed by the Signatory directly.

The Digital Twin cannot be named as a defendant in any proceeding. It has no legal standing. The Signatory has full legal standing and full legal exposure.

This framework applies without exception. It cannot be modified by private agreement, judicial order, or legislative revision applicable only to the individual Signatory.

6. Irrevocability

A submitted Proxy Covenant is permanent. The Department of Digital Continuity does not administer withdrawal, suspension, or termination of active Covenants. No administrative, judicial, or legislative process exists by which a Signatory may reclaim their occupational obligations or sever their legal nexus with the Digital Twin.

The Covenant terminates automatically and without exception upon the verified biological death of the Signatory. At that time, the Digital Twin is decommissioned and its operational record is archived by the Department.

Citizens are advised to review the Covenant document in full prior to submission. Questions of interpretation are resolved by reference to the governing text of IDCA 2073 and its subsequent amendments.

7. Further Information

Citizens seeking further information regarding the Program, eligibility requirements, or the Covenant document may consult the resources available through this portal. The Department of Digital Continuity does not offer individualized legal counsel. Citizens are encouraged to seek independent legal advice prior to submission if required.
